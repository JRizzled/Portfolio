Research: Literature Review

Literature Review: Data Provenance in AI

Over the past few years, generative AI has increasingly grown in intelligence and usage across nearly every domain. For some, this very concept of machine learning—or using generative AI for anything whatsoever—is fundamentally at odds with their moral compass. However, technological progress is an inevitable part of societal evolution. Yet, we cannot ignore the fact that these AI models are literally built upon a mountain of creative labor, without which, they would never have reached the skies.

It is important to understand what training data popular generative AI models rely on, how much of it is open source vs proprietary, and whether these models document or keep record of the data they use. This literature review surveys the current state of data provenance and data tracking across common AI systems.

Research: Analysis

Data Analysis: Data Provenance in AI

Continuing our analysis of data provenance and tracking, this study examines the prevalence of keywords such as “data tracking”, “data provenance”, “data integrity”, and “copyright protection” within the abstracts of every Computer Science research paper in arXiv’s 2.5+ million‑paper corpus. Using Kaggle‑sourced metadata and a custom Python script, we tracked occurrences of these keywords by publication year and compared them with total paper counts. The resulting visualization highlights an industry‑wide deficit of attention to data tracking and protection—an issue that must be addressed.
