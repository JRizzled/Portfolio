Research

Literature Review: Data Provenance in AI

Over the past few years, generative AI has increasingly grown in intelligence and usage across nearly every domain. For some, this itself, the very concept of machine learning or using generative AI for anything whatsoever is fundamentally at odds with their moral compass. However, I realize that technological progress is an inevitable part of societal evolution. Yet, we cannot ignore the fact that these AI models are literally built upon a mountain of creative labor, without which, they would never have reached the skies. It is only this mountain that has allowed companies like OpenAI, Google, Meta to reach the skies that they currently reside in. It is important for us to understand what training data do popular generative AI Models rely on, how much of it is open source vs proprietary, do these AI Models document or keep record of what data they are using. This literature review will look at the current state of data provenance and data tracking of common AI models.

Data Analysis: Data Provenance in AI

Continuing our analysis of data provenance and tracking, in this study we look at the prevalence of specific keywords like "data tracking", "data provenance", "data integrity", "copyright protection", etc. in the abstract of all Computer Science research found on arXiv's massive 2.5+ million paper repository. This was done with the help of a dataset of all the research paper metadata which was found on kaggle. The graph on the left was produced by my python script that tracked the number papers containing these keywords in the data set and comparing it with the total number of papers published during that year. It is clear that there is a lack of interest in protecting and tracking data and this needs to improve.
